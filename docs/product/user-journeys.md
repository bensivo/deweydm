# Dewey - User Journeys

## Overview
User Journeys give us a narrative for user experience, helping as a guiding light during development. They are useful tools for deciding whether a feature is 'good enough' for our users, whether the design is intuitive enough, or whether a feature is 100% necessary, or nice-to-have. 

## UJ-1 Project Management
Alice is a freelance designer juggling tons of projects with different clients. She uses Dewey for project management.
1. Alice goes into Dewey and starts with an empty workspace
2. She is prompted with 'Empty workspace, follow setup guide?' and she hits yes
3. Through the setup guide, she creates 3 differnet entities: Client, Project, Task. Configuring them appropriately. 
4. She uses the 'bulk create' view in each entity to quickly insert records about all her existing clients, projects
5. She then creates a basic workflow, mapped to a the project 'status' field "discovery, design, design-reveiw, implementation, implementation-review, closed"
6. She creates a 'view' for day-to-day work, which shows her current open projects, mapped to this workflow
6. When she starts work for the day, she looks at her overall project view, selects a project, then sees what tasks are pending for it. If necessary, she creates more tasks. 
7. As she works, she closes some tasks, and moves the project from left to right.
